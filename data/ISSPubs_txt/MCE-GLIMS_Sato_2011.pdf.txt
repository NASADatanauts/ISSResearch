SiX—channel spectrophotometers (PH) are the science instruments of J EM-GLIMS to measure absolute in-

tensity of the emission originated from lightning discharges and upper atmospheric transient luminous events

(TLES). PH unit—l (PHuUl) consists of four spectrophotometer channels named from PHI to PH4, while

PH unit—2 (PH—U2) two spectrophotometer channels named PH5 and PHG. Optical ﬁlters of these spec—

I trophotometers are selected to detect TLE emission lines of N2 1PG, N2 2PG, Net 1NG, and N2 LBH. Since

| the bandwidth of the optical ﬁlter of 131-12, 3, 5, and 6 is 10 nm and since PHl measures NUV emission,

photomultiplier tubes with high~voltage converters are used as a photon detector. T0 the contrary, PH4

uses a photodiode as a photon detector because the pass—band 0f the optical ﬁlter is enough wide to detect

transient optical emission. Though PH does not equip spatial resolution, it can acquire light curve data with

a high time resolution of 50 Ms with a 12—bit resolution. Thus, the combinational analysis of PH data and

Lightning and Sprite Imager (LSI) data, it is possible to clarify the relationship between TLEs and their

parent lightning discharges, the occurrence condition of TLEs, and the energy of the electrons which excite

TLE emission.
